This is my
story

From crafting innovative marketing strategies and driving financial inclusion in rural Indian villages to hopping across the globe to learn how to use data to make smarter business moves.

I grew up in India, where I completed most of my education and gained professional experience. I earned a Bachelor of Technology in Electronics and Communication and an MBA in Finance as part of a dual-degree program in 2015. After graduating, I chose the challenging path of preparing for national-level government banking exams instead of joining a corporate job. This decision was tough due to the high difficulty level and the fierce competition, with 2.5-3 million candidates vying for 7,000-9,000 vacancies annually. After rigorous preparation, I received offer letters from two national banks. I initially worked as a Deputy Manager at the Bank of Maharashtra for nine months before joining India Post Payments Bank (IPPB). IPPB, a government startup, aimed to convert post office branches into bank branches. Despite knowing the challenges of building the business from the ground up, managing operations across 190 post office branches, and persuading the rural population to adopt banking services in a saturated market, I chose to join IPPB. I was driven by a desire to serve the underserved rural population and saw it as a valuable learning opportunity early in my career.

I worked at India Post Payments Bank (IPPB) for 4.5 years as an Assistant Manager in Strategy and Marketing. In this role, I oversaw various domains including banking operations, strategic marketing, business development, and staff training programs across 190 Post Office branches in the Etah district. I played a crucial role in scaling the business and establishing IPPB’s services. Early in my tenure, I noticed the stark contrast between the financial services available in urban areas, where I was from, and the lack of basic financial services and knowledge in rural areas. Witnessing the impact of our decisions on people’s lives, I made it my mission to serve the underserved rural population. At IPPB, I worked with extensive databases from various government departments, but I realized I needed to enhance my skills to make better use of this data. After 4.5 years of dedicated service, I believed I had given my good youthful years to the nation building in true sense. I decided to return to the classroom to learn more about data analytics in Marketing domain. I earned a Master's in Marketing Analytics from the University of Maryland, College Park. My time at UMD was enriching, allowing me to explore different aspects of marketing and data analytics with the guidance of world-class faculty and diverse peers. This experience compelled me to evolve into a better version of myself.
